# Business Context

IronRidge Contractors has grown through strong execution and trusted relationships, but internal reporting maturity has lagged behind operational complexity. The company now spans multiple divisions, geographies, and work types, while many status updates still move through spreadsheets, calls, and meeting notes.

## Current Realities

- project status is often assembled manually from PM-owned trackers
- service work is managed differently across teams and regions
- closeout readiness becomes visible late unless someone chases it directly
- field conditions are not always reflected cleanly in leadership reporting
- action follow-through can affect delivery before it shows up in a formal review

## Why Leadership Cares

Leadership needs faster answers to a small set of practical questions: which projects need attention now, where service backlog is building, which closeout packages are slipping, and where field or execution risk is starting to accumulate.
