# Business Context

IronRidge Contractors has grown into a more complex operating business than its reporting habits were originally built to support.

The company now spans multiple divisions, regions, project types, and support functions. But many important updates still move through spreadsheets, calls, inboxes, and meeting notes. That makes it harder than it should be to get a fast, reliable picture of operational health.

## Current Realities

- project updates are often managed in separate PM-owned trackers
- service work is not always rolled up the same way across teams
- closeout pressure tends to become obvious later than anyone wants
- field issues do not always show up clearly in leadership reporting
- action follow-through can affect delivery before it is visible at the reporting level

## Why Leadership Cares

Leadership is not looking for more dashboards just to have more dashboards.

They need quick, practical answers to a few important questions:
- which projects need attention right now
- where service backlog is building
- which closeout items are slipping
- where risk is starting to accumulate before it becomes a bigger problem

That is the gap this repo is built to illustrate.
